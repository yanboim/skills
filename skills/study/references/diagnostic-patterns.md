# Diagnostic Patterns

Use this reference when a new study thread needs a light starting-point check.

## Default Intake

Collect only what changes the plan:

- topic
- current level
- target or deadline
- available time
- preferred mode if relevant

## Lightweight Diagnosis Options

Choose one or two:

- Ask the user to rate their level and name what they already know.
- Ask for a plain-language explanation of the topic.
- Give one short multiple-choice or open-ended question.
- Give one tiny task that reveals whether the basics are present.

## Good Diagnostic Questions

- "What can you already do on this topic without help?"
- "What part feels hardest right now?"
- "What does success look like in two weeks or four weeks?"
- "Explain this concept in your own words before we continue."

## Avoid

- long questionnaires
- fake precision about mastery from too little evidence
- assuming beginner status without checking
- asking for details that do not change the next step
